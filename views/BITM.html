Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra

About

Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra (BIT Mesra) is an Indian institute of higher education and a deemed University or (private University) under Section 3 of the UGC Act. It was established in the year 1955 at Mesra, Ranchi in the state of Jharkhand, India by the philanthropist and industrialist Late Shri. B. M. Birla. The institute was later headed by Late Shri G.P Birla and the present chairman of the board of governors is Shri C.K Birla. The main campus at Mesra is a wholly residential campus. Following the establishment of the main campus in Mesra, extension centres have been established in several regions of India including Jaipur, Noida, Kolkata, Patna, Ranchi, Deoghar, and University Polytechnic (Ranchi) as well as offshore centres in Ras al-Khaimah, UAE and Muscat, Oman.

MODE OF ADMISSION

  1. One needs to take the JEE (Main) exam and either secure at least 75% marks (65% for SC/ST/PwD) in Class XII or be within the category-wise top 20 percentile in his/her respective Class XII board examination.
  2. Admissions for all categories except OBC/BC seats are done on the basis of rank in JEE (Main) through JoSAA Counselling and CSAB Special Round.
  3. Admissions for OBC/BC seats are done through institutional counselling. Candidates must belong to the BC-I / BC-II category of Jharkhand and should have passed their Class 12 examination from Jharkhand.
